# Sample 01 — Landing page: H1, subhead, first paragraph

**Surface:** marketing site, above the fold.
**Voice check:** grade 8 max. No banned words. Consistent with PRD §1 one-liner.
**Reading level:** H1 grade 6 · subhead grade 7 · body grade 8.

---

## H1 (locked — matches PRD §1)

> **Your AI builder shipped code that fails accessibility. We'll prove it — line by line.**

13 words. Grade 6. Names the pain. Names the wedge. Names the receipt.

---

## Subhead

> Studio Zero is the independent audit for AI-built software. Connect your repo, pick a depth, get a graded checklist — every finding with a file path, a line range, and a fix.

35 words. Grade 7. Three verbs the reader recognizes. Three things every finding ships with.

---

## Below-fold first paragraph

> Our gate is strict on purpose. Most first audits do not pass it — and that is the point. A PASS from Studio Zero means a real PASS: a deterministic score, a reviewed-by-name finding list, and evidence you can hand to your team. Run your first Surface audit free.

54 words. Grade 8. Pre-empts the "why did I fail?" emotion before the customer even runs the audit. Closes with the offer.

---

## Microcopy near the primary CTA

> Free Surface audit on a URL you own. No credit card. Email verification required.

Substantiates the free tier per PRD §12, names the abuse safeguard without engineering jargon ("Email verification required" not "Email-verification required, IP-rate-limited").

---

## Notes for Pixel + Vega

- The H1 sets the page's emotional register. Subhead and body must not dilute it with adjectives.
- Do not pair the H1 with stock photography of laptops, code on monitors, or "happy team" imagery. Pair with the verdict screen itself — the product is the proof.
- Do not add a second CTA above the fold. One decision per surface.
